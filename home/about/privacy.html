Privacy Policy

This website (cyberaxar.github.io) is owned and maintained by Cyber Axar, an independent author and developer. Your privacy is important, and I want to be transparent about how this website works and what information is (and is not) collected.

This is a static website, meaning it is built with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript only. There is no backend server, no database, and no tracking code created by me. I do not collect, store, process, or share any personal information about visitors. You can browse all pages freely without providing any personal details.

This website is hosted for free on GitHub Pages. While I personally do not collect your data, GitHub (a Microsoft-owned service) may automatically collect certain technical information such as your IP address, browser type, operating system, and access times as part of their standard hosting process. This information is handled according to GitHub’s own privacy practices. For full details, you should review their privacy policy here: Read.

At present, there are no cookies, analytics scripts, or third-party trackers embedded in this website. However, in the future, I may introduce JavaScript-based features that store small amounts of data locally in your browser (known as local storage) purely to improve your browsing experience — for example, saving your theme preference or remembering your last reading position in an article. This local data will remain entirely on your device and will never be transmitted to me or to any server.

Since I am not collecting any personal data, there is nothing to sell, rent, or share with third parties. This privacy approach is intentional to keep your browsing safe, private, and distraction-free.

By using this website, you acknowledge and agree that any data collected about your visit is handled solely by GitHub according to their policies, not by me. If you have concerns about your privacy while visiting this site, I recommend using privacy-focused browser settings or reviewing GitHub’s documentation to understand their hosting-related data collection.

If this policy changes in the future, any updates will be clearly posted on this page with the date of modification. I encourage you to check back occasionally to stay informed.
